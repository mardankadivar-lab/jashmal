# Lumen Tacens — "Luz que calla"

**Philosophy:** Radiance held inside stillness. A single warm light born from the
center of darkness, never breaking the silence around it — only glowing. The
chashmal of Ezekiel: amber-electrum gleaming *from the midst of the fire*, where
speech and silence are one substance.

**Space and form.** The composition breathes around a single luminous core. Vast
negative darkness is not emptiness but reverent space — the silence (*jash*) that
lets the light (*mal*) be heard. Forms are perfect, patient circles and softened
drops: the gem, the halo, the orbit. Nothing is busy; every shape is the product
of countless refinements until only the essential remains.

**Color and material.** A near-black ground (#05050a) holds molten amber and
electrum — warm gold (#c9a43e) deepening to ember-orange (#d98a3d) at the heart,
lifting to soft highlight (#e0c873) at the rim. The amber must feel *lit from
within*, like resin with a trapped spark or metal at the edge of glowing. Cream
(#e8e4d8) text whispers. Candlelight in a study, never neon.

**Scale and rhythm.** One dominant mark, surrounded by quieter echoes —
concentric rings, faint orbiting sparks (nitzotzot), a measured cadence of light
fading into dark. Repetition is meditative: each ring dimmer, each spark smaller,
like the decay of a struck note into silence.

**Composition and hierarchy.** Symmetry on a vertical axis gives sacred stillness.
The mark sits like an icon; the wordmark rests beneath in elegant Roman capitals,
the Hebrew smaller, the tagline a faint line below. Light → name → tongue →
whisper. Master-level execution shows in the haloing, the caustics inside the gem,
the precise optical centering — painstaking attention that reads as effortless calm.

**Spirit.** The work should look labored over for countless hours by someone at
the top of their craft, yet feel utterly quiet — proof that silence can hold a
voice, and darkness can carry light.
