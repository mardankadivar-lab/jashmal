Redesign the Jashmal Tree of Life page into a cinematic sacred interactive experience.

The current page explains the 10 Sefirot and 22 paths, with click interactions and zoom/descend behavior. Keep that functionality, but transform the visual language from a technical diagram into a mystical Apple-style scrollytelling interface.

Visual direction:
- Pure black cosmic background.
- Subtle gold, white, sapphire and amber light.
- The Tree of Life appears as a living luminous structure.
- Each Sefira is a glowing sphere, slowly breathing.
- The 22 paths are thin luminous fiber-optic lines.
- Very subtle particles float in the background.
- Hebrew letters appear faintly, almost hidden, like sacred inscriptions.
- No clutter. The Tree must remain the protagonist.

Opening sequence:
1. Start with a black screen.
2. Show חַשְׁמַל in the center.
3. Split it subtly into חַשׁ / silence and מל / speech.
4. Fade into the phrase: “From silence, the Tree speaks.”
5. Reveal עֵץ חַיִּים and then the Tree of Life.

Hero copy:
עֵץ חַיִּים
The Living Tree

You are not looking at a diagram.
You are entering the hidden architecture of creation.

From Ein Sof descend the Sefirot.
From the Sefirot emerge the paths.
From the paths awaken the letters.
And in every letter, a gate.

CTA:
Enter the Tree

Interaction:
- Hovering a Sefira makes it glow and shows its Hebrew name, transliteration, and one poetic sentence.
- Clicking a Sefira opens rays of textual references: Torah, Zohar, Etz Chaim, Chasidut, Personal Application.
- Each ray should animate outward from the Sefira.
- The user can click “Enter this Sefira” to zoom into an inner Tree, because each Sefira contains all 10 Sefirot.
- Keep breadcrumb navigation: Ein Sof → Tree of Life → Keter → Inner Tree.

Design style:
- Apple-style minimalism.
- Sacred geometry.
- Kabbalistic cosmic atmosphere.
- Elegant typography.
- Smooth Framer Motion animations.
- Responsive mobile-first layout.
- No heavy paragraphs on the first screen.
- Use progressive disclosure: reveal depth only after interaction.

Technical preference:
- React / Next.js.
- SVG for the Tree structure.
- Framer Motion for transitions.
- Canvas or lightweight particle background if performance allows.
- Keep accessibility labels for each Sefira.
